Where to sleep?

There are many available options in terms of accommodation in Ulaanbaatar. It is up to your budget. However, if you decide to travel there one day, I highly recommend you to experience "ger". Ger is a traditional nomadic portable and round tent.

  1. Guest Houses+

    //Lotus Guest House

    This guesthouse is different from others because it is owned by the Lotus Childrenâ€™s Centre. The main purpose of this initiative is to give teenagers professional experience in tourism and hospitality and help them build an independent lifestyle after leaving the orphanage.

    //Chuka's Guest House

    Another attractive centrally located establishment. It is family owned and the service is decent quality considering the cost. Highly recommended!

  2. Hostels+

    // Golden Gobi

    Probably one of the busiest hostels in town, it is known to be a hub of backpackers. They have a friendly service and the hostel looked fairly clean considering its relatively low room prices.

    //Idre Hostel

    This hostel has been active since 1996 and is known to be one of the first to provide budget accommodation and package tours for independent travelers in Mongolia.

  3. Hotels+

    //Ulaanbaatar Hotel

    The very first western standard hotel of Ulaanbaatar. It was established back in 1961 when Mongolia was still a Socialist country.

    //Shangri-La Hotels and Resorts

    For those seeking for a luxurious sanctuary, this is the perfect spot.

  4. Ger Camps+

    Ger camps are undeniably 'must' experience when it comes to visiting Mongolia. Unfortunately in the capital there is very few authentic ger camp options. I'd rather suggest you to book some nights in a ger camp when you are in the steppe. Here I leave some photos for you to imagine how it is going to look like...a night in a ger.
